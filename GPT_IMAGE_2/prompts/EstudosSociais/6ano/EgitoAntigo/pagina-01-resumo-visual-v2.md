================================================================
OVERALL AESTHETIC — MAJOR DIRECTION
================================================================
Editorial-documentary infographic in the spirit of a National Geographic / Newsweek special-issue spread, mixed with a high-end magazine annotated visual essay. The page is DENSE, TACTILE, and LAYERED:

- Photorealistic painterly editorial illustrations serve as the visual anchors (focal heroes).
- Around the heroes, a strong graphic system of: COLOR BLOCKS, BRUSH-STROKE SECTION HEADERS, DASHED POINTER LINES with chip-labels, MARKER HIGHLIGHTS on key words, PICTOGRAM ICONS, and small CALLOUT CHIPS.
- Information is ANNOTATED on top of imagery — not isolated in generic boxes.
- Clean cream background `#F4EFE3` everywhere. White `#FFFFFF` only inside cards/insets.
- Hairlines 0.5px graphite. Cards rounded 4–6px. Pills fully rounded.
- NO drop shadows. NO gradients. NO glow. NO 3D. NO halftone dots covering large areas. NO neon. Slight paper grain may exist subtly only over photo painterly areas.
- Hierarchy by COLOR CONTRAST, TYPOGRAPHIC WEIGHT, and ANNOTATION LAYERING.

================================================================
TYPOGRAPHY
================================================================
- TITLES: ELEGANT DISPLAY SERIF (Playfair / Canela / GT Sectra), high-contrast, MIXED CASE.
- SUBTITLES: clean geometric sans-serif (Inter / Söhne), light, graphite.
- BODY + BULLETS: same sans, regular, graphite.
- LABELS / EYEBROWS: sans UPPERCASE with WIDE letter-tracking (~12% tracking).
- NUMBERS 01-04: serif italic, large, in the card's accent color.
- ANNOTATION CHIP LABELS: tiny sans uppercase wide-tracked.
- DATE/CHIP CAPTIONS: tiny serif italic graphite.

================================================================
DECORATION SYSTEM (used throughout)
================================================================
- BRUSH-STROKE band: a thin uneven horizontal swipe of color (terracota, forest, slate or wine) ~6px tall, semi-irregular edges (like a real brush), placed BEHIND each section eyebrow label. Like a textured underline that the label sits on top of.
- DASHED POINTER LINE: thin 0.5px graphite dash, ending in a tiny filled circle near the photo element, with a chip-label at the other end.
- CALLOUT CHIP: small white rounded pill with 0.5px graphite border, containing tiny serif italic or sans uppercase text.
- MARKER HIGHLIGHT: an irregular semi-transparent terracota/wine rectangle (30% opacity) painted BEHIND a key word in the body — like a real highlighter pen stroke (slightly off-axis, ragged edges).
- TINY PICTOGRAMS scattered as marginalia: reed, ankh, sun disk, eye of Horus, scarab — all 1px graphite outline, small, used as decorative anchors near sections.
